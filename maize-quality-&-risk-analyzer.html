Project Overview

Developer: Nhambe DB

Tool: Microsoft Excel (Advanced Logic & Data Modeling)

Sector: Agribusiness / Quality Control

Executive Summary

In regional grain storage facilities, the lack of standardized quality assessment often leads to subjective decision-making, resulting in grain spoilage and financial loss. This project provides a standardized, objective framework for Quality Control Clerks to assess grain batches at the point of intake.

Problem Statement

Before the implementation of this tool, there was no uniform method for calculating risk. Decisions to accept or reject maize were often inconsistent, increasing the risk of mold contamination in silos and reducing customer trust in the final product.

Solution & Technical Implementation

  • Weighted Risk Engine: Scoring system based on National Agricultural Weights evaluating seven critical metrics, including Moisture (%), Aflatoxin (ppb), and Insect Damage.
  • Automated Decision Logic: “Accept/Reject” logic gate where thresholds (e.g., moisture > 14%) trigger high-risk status automatically.
  • Farmer Performance Tracking: Lookup system to view specific farmer results for comparisons across farms.

Key Impact

  • Waste Reduction: Lowers probability of grain spoilage by blocking high-risk batches at intake.
  • Data-Driven Extension: Provides provincial managers with clear data for targeted educational interventions.
  • Operational Speed: Streamlines intake process for faster throughput during peak harvest seasons.
